The Client

A big idea, and no map to get there.

Haneen came to LeadStrong.ai with something most founders would kill for: a working product idea for Lumii, a small companion device built to help people feel a little less alone. What she didn't have yet was a brand, a digital presence, a way to test the idea with real people, or a plan for turning interest into a launch.

Lumii isn't a therapist, a medical device, or a replacement for human connection. It's something simpler and harder to build well: a calm, honest presence for everyday moments.

So the go-to-market had to match the product — no hype, no fear-based marketing, no overselling the AI. Just clarity, honesty, and proof.

Phase 01

Test the market before building the product.

Before a single ad ran to sell anything, LeadStrong.ai used paid social to ask people what they actually thought. Early campaigns weren't pitching a finished product — they were gathering honest, sometimes uncomfortable, reactions.

That feedback shaped Prototype 1, and the lessons from real users shaped Prototype 2 — the version Lumii is building toward today. Selling less and listening more, early, is what let Lumii avoid building something nobody wanted.

Early market-testing ad — inviting honest reactions rather than selling a finished product.

A second validation ad, positioning Lumii against app fatigue and gathering signal before scaling spend.

Phase 02

Build the brand and the home for it.

With real feedback in hand, LeadStrong.ai built Lumii's full brand identity — soft blue and white, warm and human, deliberately far from typical "AI gadget" marketing — and a website designed around one job: explain clearly what Lumii is, and what it isn't, and turn interest into waitlist signups.

Phase 03

Paid social that builds trust, not just traffic.

Once the brand and website were live, LeadStrong.ai moved into always-on Meta campaigns — Facebook and Instagram — built around Lumii's own What / Why / Proof framework. Every ad leads with clarity, taps into why it matters, and lets real product moments convince instead of aggressive sales language.
